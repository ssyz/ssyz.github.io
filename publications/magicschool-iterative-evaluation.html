Benchmark Note #2

MagicSchool improves grade-level accuracy of Informational Texts tool through iterative evaluation

March 2026

Evaluation Framework: OurDojo Grade Level Appropriateness Evaluator

Contents

  • Context
  • Results
  • Interpretation
  • Diagnostic Insight
  • Conclusion

Context

The first benchmark for MagicSchool's Informational Texts generation tool was completed in February 2026 on 100 passages and 3rd-grade target band accuracy. In collaboration with MagicSchool, OurDojo re-ran the same analysis in March in order to quantify the impact of context and prompt engineering efforts by the MagicSchool team.

This report demonstrates how MagicSchool leveraged standardized evaluation of AI in education to drive systematic improvement of product quality. The characteristics of the quality gains we uncovered underscore the importance of evaluation as an ongoing cycle of analysis, measurement, and improvement; not a one-time task.

Results

Each of the 100 passages was evaluated independently using the same standardized rubric and evaluation prompts used in the February benchmark, ensuring comparability between runs. Performance metrics changed as follows:

Feb 2026 Mar 2026
Target Band Accuracy 66% 71%
Science 35% 52%
Social Studies 87% 83%

Of the 100 generated passages evaluated, 71 fell within the target grade band of 2nd–3rd grade. Among the remaining passages, 28 were rated in the 4th–5th grade band and one was rated in the 6th–8th grade band. Several notable failure modes remained consistent across both evaluations. In particular, all failures skewed too hard and the weakest domain remained Science > Life Cycles & Traits.

Interpretation

The measurable and observable quality improvement of MagicSchool's Informational Texts generation tool deserves recognition. Overall accuracy improved by +5 percentage points and the lowest performing subject improved by almost +20 percentage points. Indeed, this practical sequence of (1) independent evaluation, (2) targeted software change, (3) updated benchmark metrics confirming improvement areas, is precisely the AI quality improvement flywheel that produces verifiable evidence of sound pedagogy.

There was a slight regression in Social Studies performance, which highlights a critical point: evaluation and improvement of AI is iterative and dynamic. Due to the non-deterministic behavior of LLMs, robust evaluation processes must be put in place in order to continuously increase quality and monitor changes across various dimensions.

Diagnostic Insight

Across both evaluations, every grade-level miss (34 in February and 29 in March) occurred above the target band, with no passages rated below grade level. This consistent pattern indicates a systematic bias towards complexity. The observed upward bias is most pronounced for Science text generation, which suggests that prompts in that pipeline may need Science-specific simplification guidance like more scaffolding for technical terms.

Conclusion

MagicSchool reduced grade complexity drift from its Informational Texts generation tool by up to 17 percentage points after implementing targeted system changes. This methodology is generalizable—standardized evaluations provide credible third-party evidence that product changes translated into measurable gains in instructional quality. OurDojo's work with MagicSchool exemplifies how standardized evaluation and benchmarking leads to quantifiable wins, while providing deeper diagnostic insights. Continuous evaluation of AI in education is essential for improving product quality and building trust between software providers and teachers.
